Mediterranean Diet — Principles and Evidence

The Mediterranean diet is consistently ranked as one of the healthiest dietary patterns in the world, with strong evidence for cardiovascular protection, longevity, and reduced risk of metabolic disease.

Core food groups:
- Olive oil as the primary fat source (3-4 tablespoons daily). Extra-virgin olive oil provides polyphenols including oleocanthal, which has anti-inflammatory properties.
- Vegetables: 3-6 servings daily. Focus on tomatoes, leafy greens, peppers, eggplant, zucchini, artichokes, and onions.
- Fruit: 2-3 servings daily. Figs, grapes, pomegranate, and citrus are traditional choices.
- Whole grains: Whole wheat bread, bulgur, farro, and brown rice. Limit refined grains.
- Legumes: Lentils, chickpeas, and fava beans 3-4 times per week — excellent protein and fibre source.
- Fish and seafood: At least 2 portions per week. Fatty fish like salmon, sardines, mackerel provide omega-3s (EPA + DHA).
- Nuts: A small handful daily (walnuts, almonds, hazelnuts). Rich in healthy fats and antioxidants.
- Dairy: Moderate amounts of cheese (especially feta and parmesan) and yogurt. Limit red meat.
- Red meat: Maximum 1-2 times per month.
- Wine: Optional, in moderate amounts with meals (1 glass/day for women, up to 2 for men).
- Herbs and spices: Garlic, basil, oregano, rosemary, thyme — reduce need for added salt.

Macronutrient profile:
Carbohydrates 45-55% | Fat 30-40% (mostly monounsaturated) | Protein 15-20%
Total calories typically 1800-2200 kcal/day for adults.

Key health outcomes (strong evidence):
- 30% reduction in major cardiovascular events (PREDIMED trial, 7,447 participants)
- Reduced risk of type 2 diabetes, cognitive decline, and depression
- Associated with longer telomere length (a marker of cellular ageing)
- Lower all-cause mortality in multiple meta-analyses

Weekly meal structure:
Monday: Grilled fish with roasted vegetables and olive oil, side of lentil soup
Tuesday: Whole wheat pasta with tomato sauce, vegetables, and a small amount of feta
Wednesday: Chickpea salad with cucumber, tomato, olive oil, and lemon
Thursday: Grilled chicken with tabbouleh and hummus
Friday: Baked salmon with roasted asparagus and brown rice
Saturday: Lamb kofta with Greek salad and whole pita
Sunday: Seafood paella or fish stew with vegetables

Shopping list essentials:
Extra-virgin olive oil, canned tomatoes, chickpeas, lentils, whole wheat pasta, brown rice, a variety of seasonal vegetables, lemons, fresh herbs, feta cheese, Greek yogurt, sardines and salmon (fresh or canned).

Mediterranean diet score (MedDiet Score):
Ranges from 0-55. A score above 28 is associated with significant cardiovascular benefit. Each point increase in MedDiet Score is associated with approximately 3% reduced risk of major cardiovascular events.
