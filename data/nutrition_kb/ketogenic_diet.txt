Ketogenic Diet — Principles, Macros, and Evidence

The ketogenic (keto) diet is a very low-carbohydrate, high-fat diet that shifts the body's primary fuel source from glucose to ketone bodies. Ketosis is typically achieved within 2-4 days of carbohydrate restriction.

Macronutrient targets:
- Fat: 70-80% of total calories
- Protein: 15-25% of total calories (do not exceed — excess protein is gluconeogenic)
- Carbohydrates: 5-10% of total calories — typically 20-50g net carbs per day
- Net carbs = total carbs minus dietary fibre

Mechanism:
When carbohydrate intake drops below approximately 50g/day, liver glycogen depletes within 24-48 hours. The liver then converts fatty acids into ketone bodies (beta-hydroxybutyrate, acetoacetate, acetone), which become the primary energy source for the brain and muscles.

Blood ketone levels:
- Nutritional ketosis: 0.5-3.0 mmol/L beta-hydroxybutyrate
- Deep ketosis: 3.0-5.0 mmol/L (often used for epilepsy management)
- Diabetic ketoacidosis: >10 mmol/L (dangerous, associated with type 1 diabetes)

Foods to eat on keto:
- Meat and fish: beef, chicken, pork, salmon, sardines, tuna — all unprocessed, unsauced
- Eggs: 2-4 per day is typical, excellent fat and protein ratio
- High-fat dairy: butter, heavy cream, hard cheeses, Greek yogurt (in small amounts)
- Low-carb vegetables: leafy greens, broccoli, cauliflower, zucchini, bell peppers, asparagus, mushrooms
- Avocado: excellent fat source, ~1-2 per day
- Nuts and seeds: macadamia, walnuts, almonds, chia, flax (in moderation — count carbs)
- Oils: olive oil, coconut oil, avocado oil, MCT oil

Foods to strictly avoid:
- Grains: bread, pasta, rice, oats, corn
- Sugar: all forms including honey, maple syrup, fruit juice
- High-sugar fruit: bananas, grapes, mangoes, apples (small berries are acceptable)
- Legumes: beans, lentils, chickpeas (too many carbs)
- Root vegetables: potatoes, sweet potatoes, carrots in large amounts
- Alcohol: most alcoholic beverages contain carbs; dry wine and spirits are lower-carb options

Sample daily meal plan (2000 kcal keto):
Breakfast: 3 scrambled eggs cooked in butter, 2 rashers bacon, half avocado (total ~600 kcal)
Lunch: Large salad with grilled chicken, olive oil dressing, walnuts, cucumber, feta (total ~500 kcal)
Dinner: Salmon fillet with roasted broccoli and cauliflower in olive oil (total ~600 kcal)
Snacks: Almonds, hard-boiled egg, cheese (total ~300 kcal)
Net carbs for the day: approximately 20-25g

Keto flu (first 1-2 weeks):
Common symptoms: fatigue, headache, brain fog, irritability, muscle cramps. Caused by electrolyte loss (sodium, potassium, magnesium) due to reduced insulin and glycogen depletion.
Management: increase sodium (1-2g extra/day from broth or salt), potassium (avocado, leafy greens), magnesium (nuts, seeds, or supplement 300-400mg glycinate).

Evidence for ketogenic diet:
- Strong evidence: epilepsy management (50%+ seizure reduction), short-term weight loss
- Moderate evidence: type 2 diabetes remission, PCOS symptom improvement
- Emerging evidence: certain cancers (glioblastoma), Alzheimer's prevention
- Mixed evidence: long-term cardiovascular effects, athletic performance

Protein on keto:
Target 1.2-1.7g per kg of body weight to preserve lean mass. For a 75kg person: 90-128g protein/day. This typically represents 18-25% of calories on a well-formulated keto diet.

Keto for weight loss:
Average weight loss in the first week is 1-3 kg (largely water from glycogen depletion). Long-term studies show keto is comparable to low-fat diets for weight loss at 12 months, but may give faster initial results and better adherence for some individuals.
