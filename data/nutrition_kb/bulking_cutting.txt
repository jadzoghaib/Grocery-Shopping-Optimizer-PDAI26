Bulking and Cutting — Body Composition Cycling

Bulking and cutting are two phases of a body composition strategy widely used by athletes and bodybuilders to maximise muscle gain while minimising fat accumulation.

THE BULK PHASE — Building muscle

Caloric surplus:
A caloric surplus of 200-500 kcal above TDEE is optimal for muscle growth. A "dirty bulk" (500+ kcal surplus) gains muscle faster but accumulates significantly more fat. A "lean bulk" (200-300 kcal surplus) is slower but results in a better muscle-to-fat ratio. Beginners can gain more muscle per week than advanced lifters and can afford a slightly larger surplus.

Expected rates of muscle gain:
- Beginner (0-1 year lifting): 1-1.5 kg lean mass/month
- Intermediate (1-3 years): 0.5-1 kg lean mass/month
- Advanced (3+ years): 0.25-0.5 kg lean mass/month

Protein requirements for bulking:
1.6-2.2g per kg of body weight per day. For a 75kg person: 120-165g protein/day. Protein synthesis is maximised when distributed across 3-5 meals with 30-40g per meal. Leucine threshold per meal: ~2.5-3g leucine triggers maximum MPS (muscle protein synthesis).

Best protein sources for bulking:
- Chicken breast: 31g protein per 100g, low fat
- Greek yogurt: 10g protein per 100g, casein-rich (slow-release before bed)
- Eggs: complete amino acid profile, high bioavailability
- Salmon: 20g protein + omega-3s for inflammation management
- Whey protein: fast-absorbing, ideal post-workout (20-40g)

Carbohydrates for bulking:
3-5g per kg bodyweight. Carbs fuel glycogen stores for training intensity. Best sources: oats, rice, sweet potatoes, pasta, fruit. Consume the majority around workouts (pre and post).

Sample bulk day (75kg athlete, 2800 kcal target):
Breakfast: Oats with whey protein, banana, and nut butter — 650 kcal, 40g protein
Pre-workout: Rice with chicken and broccoli — 500 kcal, 45g protein
Post-workout: Whey shake + banana — 350 kcal, 35g protein
Dinner: Salmon, sweet potato, and green salad — 600 kcal, 35g protein
Evening snack: Greek yogurt with berries — 200 kcal, 20g protein
Total: ~2800 kcal, ~175g protein

THE CUT PHASE — Losing fat while preserving muscle

Caloric deficit:
A deficit of 300-500 kcal/day minimises muscle loss. Larger deficits (500+ kcal) increase the risk of losing lean mass. A rate of 0.5-1% of bodyweight loss per week is optimal for muscle preservation.

Protein during cutting:
Increase to 2.2-3.1g per kg/day when in a caloric deficit to preserve lean mass. For a 75kg person: 165-230g protein/day. Higher protein needs because the body may catabolise muscle for energy when calories are restricted.

Key strategies to preserve muscle on a cut:
- Continue resistance training throughout the cut (stimulus to retain muscle)
- Minimise cardio volume — excessive cardio increases cortisol and catabolism
- Sleep 7-9 hours (growth hormone and testosterone support)
- Maintain carbohydrate intake around workouts even in deficit
- Consider a diet break (1-2 weeks at maintenance calories) every 8-12 weeks of cutting

Sample cut day (75kg athlete, 2200 kcal target):
Breakfast: Egg whites (5) with whole egg (1), spinach, and 1 slice wholegrain toast — 400 kcal, 40g protein
Lunch: Large salad with chicken breast, olive oil dressing, chickpeas — 500 kcal, 45g protein
Pre-workout snack: Greek yogurt with berries — 200 kcal, 18g protein
Dinner: Cod fillet, large portion of broccoli and green beans, small portion of rice — 450 kcal, 40g protein
Evening: Cottage cheese — 150 kcal, 18g protein
Total: ~2200 kcal, ~161g protein

Macronutrient split for cutting:
Protein: 35-40% | Carbs: 30-40% | Fat: 20-30%

Body fat percentage targets:
- Contest/stage ready (men): 4-6%, (women): 10-13%
- Athletic lean (men): 10-14%, (women): 18-22%
- Healthy/fitness (men): 15-20%, (women): 24-30%
- Begin a cut from: below 20% BF for men, below 28% for women (to avoid excessive deficit duration)

Mini-cut vs extended cut:
Mini-cut: 4-8 weeks, deficit 500 kcal/day. Good for removing small fat gains from a bulk.
Extended cut: 12-20 weeks, deficit 300-400 kcal/day. For significant fat loss. Include diet breaks.

Supplements for body composition:
- Creatine monohydrate: 3-5g/day. Increases strength and muscle volume. Safe and well-researched.
- Whey protein: convenient protein source. 20-40g post-workout.
- Caffeine: 3-6mg/kg 30-60 min before training. Improves performance and fat oxidation.
- Omega-3 (fish oil): 2-3g EPA+DHA daily. Reduces muscle soreness and supports recovery.
