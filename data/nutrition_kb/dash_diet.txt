DASH Diet — Dietary Approaches to Stop Hypertension

The DASH diet was originally developed to reduce high blood pressure without medication. It has since become recognised as one of the most evidence-backed dietary patterns for overall cardiovascular and metabolic health.

Core principle:
Reduce sodium intake while increasing potassium, calcium, and magnesium through whole foods. These minerals collectively lower blood pressure by relaxing blood vessel walls and improving kidney function.

Sodium targets:
- Standard DASH: ≤2,300 mg sodium/day (about 1 teaspoon of salt)
- Lower-sodium DASH: ≤1,500 mg sodium/day (target for those with hypertension or at high cardiovascular risk)
- Average American intake: ~3,400 mg/day — DASH typically cuts this in half

Caloric distribution:
Carbohydrates: 55% | Protein: 18% | Fat: 27% (low saturated fat)
Total calories: typically 2,000 kcal/day for adults (adjust to individual TDEE)

Daily servings (based on 2,000 kcal):

Grains (6-8 servings/day):
- 1 serving = 1 slice wholegrain bread, or 1/2 cup cooked rice/pasta/oats
- Emphasise whole grains: brown rice, oats, barley, whole wheat pasta
- Avoid: white bread, refined cereals, pastries

Vegetables (4-5 servings/day):
- 1 serving = 1 cup raw leafy greens, or 1/2 cup cooked vegetables
- Best choices: leafy greens (spinach, kale), tomatoes, broccoli, sweet potatoes, carrots
- High potassium: baked potato with skin (926 mg K), spinach (839 mg K/cup cooked)

Fruits (4-5 servings/day):
- 1 serving = 1 medium fruit, or 1/2 cup fresh/frozen fruit
- High potassium sources: bananas (422 mg K), oranges (237 mg K), apricots
- Caution: fruit juice removes fibre — prefer whole fruit

Low-fat dairy (2-3 servings/day):
- 1 serving = 1 cup skimmed or 1% milk, 1 cup low-fat yogurt, 1.5 oz low-fat cheese
- Excellent calcium + potassium source: skimmed milk has 322 mg calcium per cup
- Avoid: full-fat cheese, cream, butter

Lean meats, poultry, fish (≤6 oz/day total):
- Fatty fish 2x/week: salmon, mackerel, sardines (omega-3 for heart health)
- Skinless chicken and turkey preferred over red meat
- Limit red meat: maximum 2-3 times/week, choose lean cuts (sirloin, tenderloin)
- Avoid: processed meats (bacon, sausage, deli meats) — high sodium

Nuts, seeds, legumes (4-5 servings/week):
- 1 serving = 1/3 cup nuts, 2 tbsp seeds, 1/2 cup cooked legumes
- Walnuts: omega-3 ALA + magnesium
- Almonds: 76 mg magnesium per 28g serving
- Lentils and chickpeas: excellent potassium, magnesium, fibre

Fats and oils (2-3 servings/day):
- 1 serving = 1 tsp olive oil, 1 tbsp low-fat mayonnaise
- Prefer: olive oil, avocado oil, rapeseed oil (monounsaturated)
- Limit: saturated fats (butter, coconut oil, palm oil)
- Avoid: trans fats entirely

Sweets (≤5 per week):
- 1 serving = 1 tbsp sugar, 1 cup lemonade, 1/2 cup sorbet
- Use herbs, spices, lemon juice, and vinegar to flavour food instead of salt

Key nutrients and targets:
- Potassium: 4,700 mg/day (most people get 2,500 mg — DASH nearly doubles this)
- Calcium: 1,250 mg/day
- Magnesium: 500 mg/day
- Sodium: ≤2,300 mg/day (lower DASH: ≤1,500 mg/day)
- Fibre: 30 g/day

Evidence for DASH:

Blood pressure:
- DASH reduces systolic BP by 8-14 mmHg in hypertensive individuals
- Even without weight loss, DASH alone reduces systolic BP by 6 mmHg in normotensive people
- Effect size comparable to a single antihypertensive medication

Cardiovascular disease:
- 20% lower risk of cardiovascular events in multiple large observational studies
- Reduces LDL cholesterol by 8-10 mg/dL on average
- Lowers triglycerides

Diabetes:
- Reduces fasting insulin and improves insulin sensitivity
- Dietary fibre from DASH foods slows glucose absorption
- Combined with weight loss: 3-4 mmHg additional BP reduction

Kidney disease:
- DASH lowers microalbuminuria (an early marker of kidney damage)
- Caution: patients with chronic kidney disease (CKD stage 3+) may need to limit potassium and phosphorus — consult a renal dietitian

Weight management:
- DASH at a modest 500 kcal deficit produces 1-2 kg/month weight loss
- Weight loss amplifies the blood pressure benefit substantially

Sample DASH day (2,000 kcal, ~1,500 mg sodium):
Breakfast: Oats with skimmed milk, banana, and mixed berries — 450 kcal, ~100 mg sodium
Lunch: Lentil and vegetable soup (homemade, no added salt), wholegrain bread — 500 kcal, ~300 mg sodium
Afternoon snack: Apple + small handful almonds — 200 kcal, ~0 mg sodium
Dinner: Baked salmon, steamed broccoli and carrots, brown rice — 600 kcal, ~300 mg sodium
Evening: Low-fat yogurt with walnuts — 250 kcal, ~100 mg sodium
Total: ~2,000 kcal, ~800 mg sodium (well under target)

Practical tips for lowering sodium:
- Cook at home — restaurant food averages 1,000+ mg sodium per meal
- Read labels: "reduced sodium" = 25% less than regular; "low sodium" = ≤140 mg per serving
- Replace salt with: lemon juice, balsamic vinegar, garlic, onion, fresh herbs, paprika, cumin
- Rinse canned beans and vegetables to remove 40% of sodium
- Avoid: soy sauce (900 mg/tbsp), ketchup (160 mg/tbsp), most condiments

DASH vs Mediterranean diet:
Both patterns are heart-healthy. Key differences:
- DASH explicitly targets sodium limits; Mediterranean does not mandate this
- Mediterranean emphasises olive oil, fish, and moderate wine more heavily
- DASH includes more low-fat dairy
- Mediterranean includes more legumes and nuts as protein staples
Combined "MIND diet" (Mediterranean–DASH Intervention for Neurodegenerative Delay) specifically targets cognitive decline and Alzheimer's prevention.
